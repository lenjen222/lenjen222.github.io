On Resiliancy in failure and success

Well, until I can implement Jekyell and an easier blog format, I will simply put this here.In the high-speed, ever-evolving world of technology, resilience isn't just about bouncing back from failureâ€”it's also about staying grounded in success. The landscape of the tech industry is littered with stories of startups that soared to dizzying heights only to crash, as well as those that stumbled early on but later redefined industries. The common thread? Resilience. For however important resillance is when scrabbling on the lower rungs of the tech ladder, it is just as important when things are going great. Once reaching the upper echelons, the drive to improve still needs to be at the forefront of what you do. Most people, myself included, find this challenging. Homostasis corrupts everthing tech can be, and looking at yourself and admitting that corruption has taken hold is one of the hardest things I have ever had to do.
Next time, I hope to at least experience the sucess before the homstasis though, lmao.
